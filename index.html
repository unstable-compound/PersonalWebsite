About Me

My name is Curtis Lewis, I am a software engineer with an interest in a wide range of developer roles. I have a particular focus on machine learning, but I am happy in almost any capacity where I am tasked with solving problems. I am currently a student at Portland State University, where I am expected to graduate this Spring (2020).

In my free time I enjoy gardening, especially growing fresh produce to cook with. I also spend a lot of my free time trying to learn new things, sometimes for my chosen profession to keep myself sharp, and sometimes in an effort to learn outside of my field and broaden my understanding of the world around me.

Experience

For this site, I have curated my long list of jobs I've had to provide details only on those that show relevance to my professional and academic goals. However, I have been working since I was 15, full-time since I was 18, and the collective experience of fighting uphill to support myself and pursue higher education has given me more perspective and a wider range of soft skills than this list might shine a light on.

PSU

TCSS - CS333 Intro to Operating Systems

Technical Course Support Specialist

This is my current job. In this capacity I help undergraduates to understand the material they need for CS333, and help them to debug and problem solve with their homework projects.

HP Inc

Firmware Engineering Intern - MECOP

Firmware Engineer Intern

At HP Inc. I developed an end to end solution for the MSB R&D team to use for memory management on their ULE LaserJet products. The feature I developed simplified the process of managing tables in memory and reduced the codesize of the ULE product's firmware, which operates in a very constrained memory-environment. In addition, the feature enabled a vital security process to run on the ULE products.
